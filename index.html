About Me

I am a doctoral student at the University of Bern since 2022, working on AI in nutrition. I hold an MSc in Data Science with Distinction (2022) and a BSc in Information Technology with First Class Honours from Asia Pacific University, Malaysia.

Previously, I worked at SWIFT as a Systems and Application Management and Support Specialist, where I gained extensive experience in SAP Basis, SAP GRC, Linux administration, Oracle database upgrades, and enterprise IT operations.

Research

My PhD research advances AI-driven dietary assessment by integrating computer vision, deep learning, and knowledge graphs. Key contributions include food segmentation, recognition, and volume estimation pipelines, out-of-distribution detection for reliability, and the development of the Swiss Food Knowledge Graph (accepted at ACM MM’25).

Future work focuses on creating a multimodal nutrition assistant that integrates CV, LLMs, and structured knowledge to provide trustworthy, personalized dietary recommendations in clinical and real-world settings.

Achievements

  • Developed end-to-end dietary assessment pipeline with explainable AI.
  • Implemented single-image food volume estimation with sensor-based depth methods.
  • Fine-tuned SAM model for semi-automatic food segmentation and annotation tools.
  • Developed the first Swiss Food Knowledge Graph for personalized dietary guidance.
